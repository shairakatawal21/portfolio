04 — Case Study

Eli Lilly × Curium Pharma
$9.1B M&A Thesis

🥇 1st Place — Eli Lilly M&A Case Competition

Beat 15 teams from 5 universities · January 2026 · Team BioEdge

View Full Deck ↗
01

The Brief

Eli Lilly has doubled revenue to ~$59B and more than tripled net income since 2021 — driven almost entirely by its GLP-1 cardiometabolic franchise. Oncology contributes just $6.8B (~15% of revenue) vs. Roche's $27.8B. The challenge: identify the highest-value M&A target to build a durable, diversified oncology platform and close the gap on global leaders.

02

How We Found the Target

We started with Lilly, not the market. We mapped five strategic "hunting zones" — Neuroscience, Oncology/Precision Medicine, Immunology, Next-Gen Therapeutics, and AI/Digital. The selection was driven by market maturity, alignment with Lilly's existing investment, and size of the capability gap.

02 Oncology & Precision Medicine Selected
01 Neuroscience & Alzheimer's
03 Immunology & Autoimmune
04 Gene, Cell & RNA Therapeutics
05 AI & Digital Drug Discovery

Within Oncology, we focused on radiopharmaceuticals — a $6.7B market growing at 9.3% CAGR to $14.4B by 2034. The key insight: radiopharma is a platform game, not a molecule game. Winning requires end-to-end control of diagnostics, isotope supply, manufacturing, and distribution. Lilly had early radiopharma exposure via POINT Biopharma and Radionetics — but no platform.

03

Target Screening

We screened the radiopharma landscape across three capability dimensions: diagnostics & radiopharmacy operations, manufacturing infrastructure, and theranostics/radioligand therapy platform ownership.

Company Diagnostics Manufacturing Theranostics Total
Curium Pharma 99624
Novartis (AAA) 78924

ITM 37616

Curium emerged as the clear target: the largest integrated radiopharmaceutical supplier globally, with 50+ products, 14M patient doses annually across 60+ countries, and a vertically integrated platform from isotope production to last-mile delivery.

04

Valuation

Curium was recapitalized at ~$7.1B in H2 2025. We proposed a $9.1B acquisition price, reflecting a strategic premium over the recapitalization and applying a weighted-average EV/EBITDA multiple of 26.65x to 2025E EBITDA of $312M.

Telix (ASX) 66.8x EV/EBITDA
Lantheus (LNTH) 13.8x EV/EBITDA
Eckert & Ziegler 10.5x EV/EBITDA
Jubilant Pharmova 15.5x EV/EBITDA
Curium (Deal) 26.65x Weighted Avg
05

Deal Structure

We proposed a full 100% acquisition financed 70% debt / 30% cash on hand, with an earn-out component to align sponsor incentives and reduce upfront risk.

Cash at close (Sponsor) $5.9B · 80%
Earn-out (EBITDA, RLT approval, Dx-Tx launch) $1.1B · 15%
Management incentive pool $0.4B · 5%

Financing: ~$4.1B debt + $1.8B cash + $1.3M shares · Net debt/EBITDA ≈ 1.4–1.5x (<2.0x)

06

Key Synergies

Revenue Uplift

+25–35% CAGR for 3 years via Lilly's oncology salesforce accelerating Curium adoption. Benchmarked against Retevmo launch post-Loxo acquisition ($275M+ revenue, RET inhibitor category leader).

Margin Expansion

300–600 bps gross margin improvement via Lilly's payer access, hospital formulary leverage, and isotope sourcing economies. Lilly already negotiates with Medicare and major payer networks.

SG&A Leverage

10–20% SG&A reduction through shared G&A, marketing, and sales infrastructure. ~150 FTE overlap eliminated over 2 years post-close.

Capex Efficiency

10–30% capex reduction vs. standalone growth case. Curium can leverage Lilly's Indianapolis manufacturing facility (built for POINT Biopharma), avoiding duplicate radiopharma infrastructure buildout.

Result

The BioEdge team's thesis — that radiopharmaceuticals require platform ownership, not just molecule acquisition — resonated with the panel of 8 industry experts. We took 1st place over 15 competing teams from 5 universities.
